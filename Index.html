How to Rent a Vehicle

Step 1: Make a Reservation

Reservations can be made through our website, mobile app, or by calling our customer service line at 1-800-ELITE-DR. We recommend booking at least 48 hours in advance for best vehicle availability and rates, especially during peak travel seasons (summer, holidays, spring break).

Information needed for reservation:

  • Full name as it appears on driver's license
  • Valid email address and phone number
  • Pickup and drop-off locations
  • Rental dates and times
  • Preferred vehicle category
  • Any additional services or equipment needed

Step 2: Review Confirmation

After completing your reservation, you will receive a confirmation email with your reservation number, rental details, total estimated cost, and pickup instructions. Please review this information carefully and contact us immediately if any details are incorrect.

You can modify or cancel your reservation up to 24 hours before the scheduled pickup time without any cancellation fee through our website or mobile app.

Step 3: Vehicle Pickup

Required documents at pickup:

  • Valid driver's license held for at least 1 year (international customers need both foreign license and International Driving Permit)
  • Major credit card in the renter's name (debit cards accepted with additional requirements)
  • Reservation confirmation number
  • Proof of insurance if declining our coverage options

Our rental counter staff will review your reservation, verify your documents, explain insurance options, and complete the rental agreement. A pre-authorization hold will be placed on your credit card for the estimated rental charges plus a security deposit (typically $200-500 depending on vehicle class).

Before leaving the lot, inspect the vehicle with a staff member, noting any existing damage on the rental agreement. Take photos or videos for your records. Familiarize yourself with the vehicle controls, fuel type, and operation.

Step 4: During Your Rental

Keep your rental agreement and roadside assistance contact information in the vehicle at all times. If you experience any mechanical issues, contact our 24/7 roadside assistance immediately at 1-800-555-HELP. Do not attempt repairs yourself.

For accidents or damage, regardless of fault, you must contact local authorities to file a police report and notify Elite Drive within 24 hours. Failure to report accidents may affect your insurance coverage.

Step 5: Vehicle Return

Return the vehicle to the agreed-upon location by the scheduled return date and time. A 59-minute grace period is provided; after that, hourly late fees apply. For a full-day late return, you will be charged the daily rate plus applicable fees.

Return checklist:

  • Return with a full tank of fuel (use fuel type specified in rental agreement)
  • Remove all personal belongings and check trunk, glove box, and under seats
  • Return all keys, documents, and equipment (GPS, toll pass, etc.)
  • Park in designated return area
  • Complete return inspection with staff member if counter is open

For after-hours returns, park in designated area, lock the vehicle, and drop keys in secure drop box. Final inspection will occur during next business hours, and you will receive an email with final charges.

Insurance & Protection Options

Loss Damage Waiver (LDW)

Cost: $18-35/day depending on vehicle class

This waiver reduces your financial responsibility for damage to or theft of the rental vehicle. With LDW, you are not responsible for damage costs up to the vehicle's value, provided you comply with rental agreement terms. This is not insurance but a waiver of our right to hold you liable for damage.

Important exclusions: Damage from driving under the influence, off-road driving, reckless operation, or using vehicle for illegal purposes is not covered.

Supplemental Liability Insurance (SLI)

Cost: $14.99/day

Provides additional liability coverage up to $1 million for bodily injury and property damage to third parties. This supplements the state-minimum liability coverage included in your rental. Highly recommended for peace of mind, especially if your personal auto insurance does not extend to rental vehicles.

Personal Accident Insurance (PAI)

Cost: $9.99/day

Covers medical expenses for the renter and passengers in case of an accident during the rental period. Provides coverage up to $175,000 for accidental death and $2,500 for ambulance and medical expenses per person. Also includes accidental death benefit of $175,000 for the renter.

Personal Effects Coverage (PEC)

Cost: $6.99/day

Protects personal belongings in the rental vehicle against theft or damage up to $1,000 per renter and $1,500 per rental transaction. Coverage is secondary to your homeowner's or renter's insurance. Excludes cash, credit cards, and certain valuables.

Do You Already Have Coverage?

Before purchasing additional insurance, check with your personal auto insurance provider to see if rental vehicles are covered under your existing policy. Many credit cards also provide collision damage coverage for rental cars when you use the card to pay for the rental.

If you have adequate coverage through other sources, you may decline our insurance options. However, you must provide proof of insurance at the rental counter.

Premium Protection Package

Cost: $39.99/day (saves 20% versus purchasing separately)

Includes all protection options: LDW, SLI, PAI, and PEC bundled together for comprehensive coverage and peace of mind. This package is our most popular choice for customers who want complete protection.

Rental Requirements & Policies

Age Requirements

  • Minimum Age: 21 years old with valid driver's license
  • Young Driver Fee: Renters aged 21-24 must pay $25/day surcharge
  • Age 25+: No additional fees based on age
  • Senior Drivers: No maximum age limit; all drivers must present valid license
  • Vehicle Restrictions: Luxury vehicles, large SUVs, and premium categories require renter to be 25 or older

Driver's License Requirements

  • Valid, unexpired driver's license issued at least 1 year prior to rental date
  • License must be printed in Roman alphabet (English letters)
  • International customers must present both foreign license and International Driving Permit (IDP)
  • Learner's permits and temporary licenses are not accepted
  • License must show current address or you must provide proof of current residence

Payment Methods

Accepted at Pickup:

  • Major credit cards: Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover
  • Credit card must be in the primary renter's name
  • Sufficient credit available for rental charges plus security deposit ($200-500)

Debit Cards: Accepted with additional requirements including proof of return travel (round-trip airline ticket), two forms of identification, proof of residence within 50 miles of rental location, and additional credit check. Debit cards are not accepted for luxury or premium vehicles.

Cash: Not accepted at pickup. Cash deposits can be used for payment at return only if rental was originally secured with a credit card.

Prepaid Cards: Not accepted under any circumstances.

Additional Driver Policy

Additional drivers must meet the same age and license requirements as the primary renter. Fee is $15/day per additional driver. The following are exempt from additional driver fees:

  • Spouse or domestic partner of primary renter
  • Business colleague of primary renter when traveling for company business (must provide proof)
  • Elite Drive Rewards Elite members can add one additional driver free of charge

All additional drivers must be present at pickup to present their driver's license and sign the rental agreement. Only authorized drivers listed on the rental agreement are permitted to drive the vehicle.

Mileage Policy

Unlimited Mileage: All rentals within the continental United States include unlimited mileage at no extra charge.

Alaska and Hawaii: Rentals limited to 100 free miles per day; $0.25 per mile for additional mileage.

International Travel: Crossing into Mexico requires Mexican insurance purchased through Elite Drive ($45/day). Canadian travel permitted with 48-hour advance notice and proof of additional insurance.

Fuel Policy

Full-to-Full: Vehicle is provided with a full tank and must be returned with a full tank. Fuel type (regular, premium, diesel) is specified on rental agreement and key tag.

Failure to Refuel: If returned without full tank, you will be charged $4.99 per gallon for missing fuel plus $15 service fee.

Prepaid Fuel Option: Purchase full tank at competitive rate at pickup and return vehicle empty (no refund for unused fuel).

Electric Vehicles: Provided fully charged; must return with at least 70% charge or pay $25 charging fee plus $0.50 per percentage point below 70%.

Geographic Restrictions

  • Rentals permitted throughout the continental United States
  • Travel to Alaska and Hawaii permitted with advance notice
  • Travel to Canada permitted with 48-hour advance notice and proof of adequate insurance coverage
  • Travel to Mexico permitted with Mexican insurance coverage purchased through Elite Drive
  • Off-road driving strictly prohibited; includes unpaved roads, beaches, and trails
  • Driving on Baja California permitted only with specific authorization and insurance

Smoking and Pet Policy

Smoking: All Elite Drive vehicles are non-smoking. Smoking in the vehicle will result in a $250 cleaning and deodorization fee.

Pets: Pets are allowed in vehicles with the following conditions:

  • Pet must be secured in carrier or pet seatbelt
  • Pet damage or excessive hair/odor will result in $150-300 cleaning fee
  • Service animals always permitted at no charge
  • We recommend using a protective seat cover (available for purchase at rental counter for $19.99)

Prohibited Uses

The following uses are strictly prohibited and may void insurance coverage and result in immediate termination of rental agreement:

  • Operating vehicle under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Using vehicle for any illegal purpose or to transport illegal substances
  • Racing, competitive driving, or driver training/testing
  • Transporting more passengers than seat belts available
  • Subletting or re-renting the vehicle to others
  • Using vehicle for commercial delivery or ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft) without prior authorization
  • Towing or pushing anything unless vehicle is equipped with proper towing package
  • Off-road driving or driving on unpaved surfaces
